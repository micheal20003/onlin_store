What is TRX?

TRX exercises are a dynamic form of suspension training that uses adjustable straps and your body weight to perform a wide range of movements. Originally developed by a Navy SEAL, TRX training is designed to improve strength, balance, flexibility, and core stability—all without the need for bulky gym equipment. Whether you're just starting your fitness journey or looking to add variety to your workouts, TRX offers scalable exercises that adapt to your fitness level. By leveraging gravity and your body’s own resistance, TRX helps you engage multiple muscle groups simultaneously, making your training both efficient and effective.

Exercises

1. TRX Squat

A TRX squat is a lower body exercise that uses TRX straps to add resistance and stability. Perform TRX squats for 30 to 45 seconds to build lower body strength.

2. TRX Push-Up

The TRX Push-Up is a bodyweight exercise that targets the chest, shoulders, and core. Using suspension training equipment like the TRX trainer challenges your stability and strength.

It is excellent for building upper body and core strength.

3. TRX Row

The TRX row targets the back, upper body, and core by utilizing your body weight. It is a versatile exercise that can be performed almost anywhere, making it great for building upper body strength.

4. TRX Chest Fly

This exercise resembles a standing cable fly or pec deck fly and directly targets the chest muscles, while also engaging the upper arms as stabilizers. It is excellent for building stability and balance.

5. Pistol Squat

The pistol squat is an advanced single-leg squat using only your body weight. It requires power and control, highlighting any imbalances in your lower body.

6. TRX Push-Up (Variation)

This variation of the push-up emphasizes the pectoral muscles, triceps, and anterior deltoids. Adjusting your form can target different muscle groups for additional challenge.

7. TRX Inverted Row

The inverted row is highly effective for strengthening the back, particularly the latissimus dorsi. It complements chest-focused exercises and builds pulling strength for TRX pull-ups.

8. TRX Single-Arm Inverted Row

This exercise targets the lats and upper back by performing rows one arm at a time, ensuring balanced muscular development across both sides of the body.

9. TRX Low Row

The low row targets the upper traps, lats, and abs. It’s a great compound movement that rounds out your workout.

10. TRX Spider-Man Push-Up

This advanced push-up variation mimics Spider-Man's movement. As you lower into the push-up, drive one knee towards your elbow for a full-body challenge that enhances core strength and balance.

11. TRX Kneeling Tricep Press

Kneel on the floor and lean forward while holding the TRX handles. You can adjust your foot position to increase resistance. Experiment with different grips for variety.

12. TRX Overhead Tricep Extension

Also known as the TRX French press, this exercise involves extending your arms overhead while keeping your elbows high. Variations include rotating the hands to change the grip during the extension.

13. TRX Skull Crusher

This variation of the traditional skull crusher targets the triceps by moving your hands past your head into full flexion while maintaining shoulder-width spacing.

14. TRX Triceps Dips

TRX triceps dips allow for full hand rotation, which means you can try different grip positions. Adjusting your foot placement can modify the exercise’s intensity.

15. TRX Push-Up Feet in Strap

In this variation, your hands remain on the ground while your feet are secured in TRX foot cradles, increasing the challenge and intensity of your push-up.

16. TRX Lunge

The TRX Lunge enhances lower limb strength, endurance, and balance. This functional exercise works the joints and associated muscles of the foot/ankle, knee, and hip.

17. TRX Hamstring Curls

Ideal for beginners, this exercise uses the TRX system to engage your core and glutes as you hold a bridge position while curling your hamstrings.

18. TRX Alligator

The TRX Alligator targets the shoulders, back, and obliques. Lean back until your body forms a diagonal line and alternately pull the straps with each arm.

19. TRX Plank on Elbows

This isometric core exercise is held in a push-up-like position on your forearms, elbows, and toes. Variations such as the side or reverse plank are also effective.

20. TRX Hamstring Runner

Similar to a mountain climber, this compound exercise targets the rear chain and core. It builds stability and strength in your hamstrings while engaging your core.

Benefits of TRX Training

  • Improve mobility and stability
  • Increase metabolic results
  • Build lean muscle
  • Develop functional strength
  • Enhance flexibility, coordination, and balance
  • Provide a great aerobic workout
  • Offer a full-body workout
  • Strengthen core muscles
  • Reduce injury risk by improving neuromuscular activation
